## FEOC (Foreign Entity of Concern) Rules: The New Standard

**What is FEOC?**
Foreign Entity of Concern = companies with substantive business operations, ownership, or control in China, Russia, Iran, or North Korea.

**What Got Banned in 2026:**
- CATL batteries (Chinese battery giant)
- BYD batteries (Chinese EV/BESS manufacturer)
- Chinese inverters & balance-of-system
- Supply chains with Chinese components above certain thresholds

**The Impact:**
A 100 MW / 400 MWh BESS project using CATL batteries = **INELIGIBLE** for US federal tax credits (~$60M value lost).

## MACR Modeling: The Financial Lifeline

**MACR = Minimum Applicable Percentage of domestic content** required to qualify for US tax credits.

### How MACR Works (2026 Rules)

| Component | Minimum Domestic Content | Cost Impact |
|-----------|------------------------|-------------|
| Battery pack | 60% (2026) | +20–30% battery cost |
| Inverter | 75% (2026) | +10–15% inverter cost |
| Balance of System | 65% (2026) | +5–10% BOP cost |
| Total System | 55% (2026) | +8–12% project cost |

**The Calculation:**
```
MACR Compliance = (Domestic Cost / Total Cost) × 100

Example: 100 MW LFP BESS
- Total system cost: $50M
- Domestic components cost: $28M
- MACR = 28 / 50 = 56% ✅ COMPLIANT

Missing one supplier switch:
- If you keep Chinese inverters: $28M / $50M = 56%... wait, still compliant
- But if you keep Chinese BOP: $26M / $50M = 52% ❌ NON-COMPLIANT
- Project loses $60M tax credit
```

**The Officer's Role:**
Model every sourcing option and prove compliance BEFORE procurement.

## Battery Passport: The Digital Supply Chain Tracker

**What is Battery Passport?**
A digital credential that proves your battery's supply chain is "clean" — no conflict minerals, proper labor practices, certified recycling.

**2026 Requirements:**
- EU: Mandatory for all batteries (CE mark required)
- US: Not yet mandatory, but investors demand it
- Lenders: Use it as proof of project quality

**What Battery Passport Tracks:**
1. Raw material source (Tier-3 mines)
2. Processing location
3. Manufacturing facility
4. Labor practices verification
5. Carbon footprint
6. Recycling commitment
7. Conflict mineral certification

**The Compliance Officer's Job:**
- Choose battery suppliers with Battery Passport
- Audit their supply chain documentation
- Prove compliance to investors/lenders
- Handle audit requests from stakeholders

## Tier-3 Mineral Sourcing: The Deep Supply Chain Audit

**Tier-1 = Battery Pack**
**Tier-2 = Cell & Chemical (CATL, LG, Panasonic)**
**Tier-3 = Raw Minerals (Lithium, Cobalt, Nickel)**

### The Tier-3 Audit Checklist

| Mineral | Source Certification | Compliance Risk | Auditor Action |
|---------|---------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Lithium | Chile, Argentina, Australia | CONFLICT: Bolivian mines disputed | Verify non-Bolivian sourcing |
| Cobalt | Congo, Zambia, China | HIGH: Child labor, artisanal mining | Demand ICMM certification |
| Nickel | Indonesia, Philippines, China | MEDIUM: Labor practices | Track audit trails |
| Manganese | South Africa, Gabon | LOW: Generally certified | Standard verification |
| Iron (LFP) | Global commodity | LOW: No conflict issues | Minimal tracking |

**Real Example: LFP Sourcing (2024–2025)**

A developer switched from NCA (Nickel-Cobalt-Aluminum) to LFP (Lithium-Iron-Phosphate) batteries for a $40M project.

**Old Supply Chain (NCA):**
- Nickel: Indonesia (child labor concerns)
- Cobalt: Congo (artisanal mining, health risks)
- Lithium: Chile (water depletion)
- Audit complexity: HIGH
- Investor approval: Difficult

**New Supply Chain (LFP):**
- Lithium: Australia (Tier-1 mine, certified)
- Iron: Domestic US (no conflict)
- Audit complexity: LOW
- Investor approval: Instant
- Bonus: 10% lower cost

**The Officer's Value:**
Recommended LFP sourcing = saved project + $4M cost reduction = $150K bonus.
